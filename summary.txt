The 2014-2015 Annual Report for India’s Ministry of Shipping outlines the sector's critical role in handling 95% of the nation's trade volume. Key initiatives include the **Sagarmala project** for port-led development and the **Jal Marg Vikas** project for augmenting National Waterway-1.

**Key Highlights:**
*   **Ports:** 12 Major Ports handled 433.87 MT of cargo (Apr-Dec 2014), with average turnaround times improving to 2.22 days.
*   **Shipping:** The Indian fleet grew to 1,205 ships (10.31M GT). To boost competitiveness, the government implemented 100% FDI and bunker fuel tax exemptions.
*   **Capacity:** The "Maritime Agenda 2010-20" targets a total port capacity of 3,130 MT by 2020.
*   **Governance:** Under "Minimum Government, Maximum Governance," the Ministry delegated more financial powers to Port Trusts and simplified ship licensing.
*   **Cleanliness/Safety:** The report notes active participation in the Swachh Bharat Abhiyan and ongoing anti-piracy measures.

The 2015-16 plan allocation is ₹4,546.53 crore.
