The Ministry of Shipping's 2014-15 Annual Report outlines its pivotal role in India's maritime transport, encompassing ports, shipping, shipbuilding, and inland waterways, which handles 95% of the nation's trade by volume.

During April-December 2014, Indian ports achieved a 6.79% growth in cargo traffic, with major ports reporting improved turnaround times. The Ministry emphasized substantial capacity expansion targets under the "Maritime Agenda 2010-20" and re-launched the "Sagarmala" project to drive port-led development, modernize infrastructure, and improve hinterland connectivity, actively promoting private sector investment.

To enhance the competitiveness of Indian shipping, facing challenges like an aging fleet and declining share in overseas trade, the government implemented policies including 100% FDI, tonnage tax benefits, reduced service tax on coastal transport, and customs/excise duty exemptions for bunker fuels. Significant focus was also placed on developing Inland Water Transport through projects like "Jal Marg Vikas." The Ministry also enacted "Minimum Government, Maximum Governance" reforms, delegating greater powers to ports and simplifying various procedures.
